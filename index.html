RDCFace: Radial Distortion Correction for Face Recognition

He Zhao, Xianghua Ying*, Yongjie Shi, Xin Tong, Jinsi Wen, and Hongbin Zha
Accepted by IEEE Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition (CVPR), 2020

Computer Vision, Face Recognition, Distortion Correction

We propose a distortion-invariant face recognition system called RDCFace, which only utilize the distorted images of faces, to directly alleviate the effects of radial lens distortion. Extensive experiments indicate that our method is robust to distortion and gains significant improvements on several benchmarks including LFW, YTF, CFP, and RadialFace, a real distorted face dataset compared with state-of-the-art methods.

G-FAN: Graph-Based Feature Aggregation Network for Video Face Recognition

He Zhao, Yongjie Shi, Xin Tong, Jinsi Wen, and Xianghua Ying*
Submitted to British Machine Vision Conference (BMVC), 2020

Computer Vision, Video Face Recognition, Graph Convolution Networks

We propose a graph-based feature aggregation network (G-FAN) for video face recognition. G-FAN automatically learns to advocate high-quality frames while repelling low-quality ones. Experiments indicate that G-FAN significantly boosts video face recognition accuracy and outperforms other state-of-the-art aggregation methods.

Quadratic Additive Angular Margin Loss for Face Recognition

Computer Vision, Face Recognition, Loss Function

We analyze the inherent limitations of ArcFace, including the non-monotonic logit and gradient curve, and inappropriate trend of loss value To address these problems, we propose a novel loss function named the Quadratic Additive Angular Margin Loss (QAMFace). It takes the value of the angle through a quadratic function rather than cosine function as the target logit. Experiments on several relevant benchmarks show that QAMFace performs better in convergence on feature embedding, and consistently outperforms the state-of-the-art face recognition methods.

Internship

MEGVII Research Institute

Pose Robust Face Recognition

I reproduced "Pose-Robust Face Recognition via Deep Residual Equivariant Mapping", a paper of CVPR 2018 based on ArcFace. This paper used L2 loss to minimize the feature distance between frontal and profile faces. I designed several experiments and proved that L2 Loss would degrade the recognition performance. Furthermore, I use KL divergence to replace L2 Loss and outperform state-of-the-art methods on CPLFW and IJB-C datasets.
